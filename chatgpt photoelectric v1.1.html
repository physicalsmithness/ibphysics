Classical Wave Model
—
Light is a continuous wave. Energy is spread over the surface, so all electrons gain energy gradually (rate set by intensity). In this classical picture, there is no threshold frequency: given enough time, emission should eventually occur.
Photon (Particle) Model
—
Light arrives as photons. Each interaction is one photon → one electron. If hf ≥ φ, emission is immediate and the electron leaves with KE = hf − φ. If hf < φ, no photoelectrons are emitted (even at high intensity).
